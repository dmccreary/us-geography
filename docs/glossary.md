# Glossary

## A

**Appalachian Mountains** - A mountain range in eastern North America extending from Alabama to Maine.

## C

**Capital** - The city where a state or national government is located.

**Climate** - The long-term weather patterns of a region.

**Continental Divide** - The line of highest points in North America that separates watersheds draining to the Pacific Ocean from those draining to the Atlantic Ocean and Gulf of Mexico.

## G

**Great Lakes** - The five large freshwater lakes (Superior, Michigan, Huron, Erie, Ontario) located on the US-Canada border.

**Great Plains** - A broad expanse of flat grassland in central North America.

## M

**Mississippi River** - The second-longest river in North America, flowing from Minnesota to the Gulf of Mexico.

## R

**Rocky Mountains** - A major mountain range in western North America extending from New Mexico to Alaska.

## T

**Topography** - The arrangement of natural and artificial physical features of an area.
