# Core Geographic Concepts

## Overview

This chapter explores the core concepts that form the foundation of geographic study.

## Physical Geography

Physical geography examines natural features:

### Landforms

- **Mountains** - Rocky Mountains, Appalachians, Sierra Nevada
- **Plains** - Great Plains, Atlantic Coastal Plain
- **Plateaus** - Colorado Plateau, Columbia Plateau
- **Valleys** - Central Valley of California, Mississippi River Valley

### Water Features

- **Rivers** - Mississippi, Missouri, Colorado, Columbia
- **Lakes** - Great Lakes, Great Salt Lake
- **Oceans** - Atlantic, Pacific, Arctic (Alaska)
- **Gulfs** - Gulf of Mexico

### Climate Zones

The US spans multiple climate zones:

- Tropical (Hawaii, South Florida)
- Arid/Desert (Southwest)
- Mediterranean (California coast)
- Humid Subtropical (Southeast)
- Humid Continental (Northeast, Midwest)
- Subarctic/Arctic (Alaska)

## Human Geography

Human geography focuses on people and their activities:

- **Population Distribution** - Where people live and why
- **Urban vs. Rural** - Cities, suburbs, and countryside
- **Economic Activities** - Agriculture, industry, services
- **Cultural Regions** - Areas with shared traditions and characteristics

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

1. Identify major landforms and water features of the United States
2. Describe the major climate zones
3. Explain how physical geography influences human settlement
